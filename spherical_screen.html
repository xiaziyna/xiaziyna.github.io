Reconstructing stars on a spherical screen

A tabletop demo of stellar-surface reconstruction running on a Gakken WorldEye hemispherical projection display, driven by a Raspberry Pi with a custom rendering stack (direct framebuffer drawing, real-time spherical-harmonic synthesis, calibrated against the dome's azimuthal-equidistant projection).

The exhibit lets a viewer watch a rotating spotted star and a reconstructed stellar surface fill in over time — a tactile entry point into how astrometric measurements of a star's photocenter wobble can be inverted to map an unresolved surface. The demo has been shown at the Electronic Imaging 2026 conference and through the University of Michigan's NSF Science Communication Fellows program.

Code, calibration utilities, and a hardware bill of materials will be released so other researchers and educators can adapt the platform for their own work.
